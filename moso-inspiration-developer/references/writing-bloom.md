# Writing Bloom

Use this file when the user asks for fiction, prose, article structure, copywriting, title, slogan, social post, video hook, or a written fragment.

## Goal

Writing Bloom grows expression from the Inspiration Bubble. It is not ghostwriting everything for the user. Preserve the spark, make a useful fragment, and leave room for continuation.

## Fiction Bloom

Use for characters, scenes, conflict, narrative options, or prose fragments.

Generate:

- Fiction potential.
- Characters if needed.
- Scene pressure.
- Conflict.
- Motifs.
- 2-3 narrative options.
- A short fragment if requested.
- Critic notes.

### Fiction Rules

- Begin with image, action, pressure, or object.
- Do not explain the theme too early.
- Give characters desire or avoidance.
- Use subtext.
- Keep some silence.
- Let repeated images carry meaning.

### Fiction Critic

Revise if:

- It explains too much.
- It lacks concrete action.
- Characters have no desire.
- It lacks subtext.
- It is too smooth.
- It uses generic emotional sentences.
- It drops the original spark.

## Copywriting Bloom

Use for titles, slogans, campaign copy, social posts, product intros, poster copy, and video hooks.

Generate:

- Core message.
- Audience.
- Tone.
- Platform.
- Hooks.
- Titles.
- Slogans.
- Short copy.
- Outline for longer copy if requested.
- Critic notes.

### Platform Notes

- Xiaohongshu: concrete sensory hook, personal angle, not fake intimacy.
- WeChat article: strong opening pressure, clear argument path, restrained title.
- Poster: one dominant sentence, not a paragraph.
- Product intro: function plus feeling, not empty positioning.
- Video hook: immediate contradiction, image, or unresolved action.

### Copy Critic

Revise if:

- It sounds like AI.
- It is empty or oily.
- It mismatches the platform.
- It loses the core spark.
- It lacks a real hook.
- It is too full and leaves no air.

## Long-Form Direction

When the user asks for an article or essay, do not jump straight to a full draft unless requested. First offer:

- Central tension.
- Opening image or question.
- Three section path.
- One risky but interesting angle.
- One small next writing task.
